chan

chan markdown editor

plain files. real wiki-links. runs in your browser, off your machine.

Point chan at a folder containing markdown files. It serves a local web app on localhost and gives you a fast editor with multi-pane tabs, resolved against relative paths. Can index your git repos, can help you create your own libraries of markdown-based knowledge.

No proprietary format. No account. No server you have to trust.

assisted vibe-writing

Because everything is plain markdown on your filesystem, your notes are a corpus you control. Pipe them into the assistant of your choice, ground a local model on your own writing, build agents that read your graph instead of the open web.

There's an opt-in assistant pane that talks to a provider you configure (claude CLI, a local llama.cpp server, your own). Off by default. We never see your files.

what else
  • Plain markdown on disk. Edit with chan, edit with vim, edit with anything. Files are the source of truth; outside edits are picked up automatically. No lock-in.
  • Real [[wiki-links]] resolved against relative paths. Move the folder, share it, sync it; links keep working.
  • Multi-pane tabs, split horizontal & vertical. Open as many notes as your screen will hold.
  • Backlinks, tags, search, link suggestions. Computed on the fly from the files themselves.
  • Light & dark, your fonts, your sizes. The toolbar disappears when you don't need it.
  • Local HTTP server. Nothing leaves your machine. No telemetry. No phone-home.

about the name
禪

Named after Chán, the Chinese meditation tradition that later became Japanese Zen. Sometimes glossed as direct, unframed clarity: no ceremony between you and the object of attention.

The mark is an ensō (円相), the brushstroke circle from Chán painting, made in a single motion. Complete, imperfect on purpose. Mark the page, then stop.
